Info

We decided to make this website to show you the best mods for Minecraft Vanilla.
All the mods in the various folders are only compatible with Fabric and start from version 1.14 (which is the oldest version supported by Fabric) up to 1.19, until now the most recent version.


Mods description

The mods in the folders replace client mods like Badlion and Lunar and features of Optifine.
Their goal is mainly to increase the fps but still play with various useful features.

From 1.16.1 onwards there are:
a mod that shows the status of the armor;
a mod that shows your Optifine/LabyMod/Minecraftcapesmod cloak even if you are not playing with one of these mods;
a mod that shows information such as fps-coordinates-biome etc.;
a mod that eliminates fog;
a mod through which you can zoom;
a mod that increases the fps (the protagonist mod, Sodium);
a mod that shows a minimap and through which you can create waypoints.
In 1.19 a mod is also added through which you can increase the brightness in the game, since changing gamma in the game files doesn't work.
Also, there are "add-ons" necessary for the operation or configuration of mods, such as Fabric API, Mod Menu, Cloth Config, Fabric Kotlin and Malilib.

Unfortunately from 1.14 to 1.16 there are not all these mods because they are not available, since they are not such recent versions.

In some versions some mods are not there, either because they are not available or because they are incompatible with other mods.


Tutorial

Choose a version to your liking and click on it to download the mods of that version.

In the zipped folder you will find the mods folder which is the one that contains the mods: copy and paste the mods or directly drag the entire folder in the game files and you are ready to play with the best mods for Minecraft Vanilla!

Also there is another folder, which contains the links to mod websites.
On the website of a mod you can find all the information regarding that mod and choose to also download a different version of the mod from the one you just downloaded (even if the mods that are in the folder are always updated, tested and working).
